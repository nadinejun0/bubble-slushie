About the Aliquot Method

The Aliquot method involves placing a small sample of your dough in a straight-sided container to monitor fermentation progress. This sample expands at the same rate as your main dough, acting as a visual indicator of fermentation progress.

How to use the Aliquot jar:

  1. Take a small sample (as recommended by calculator) of final dough after completing stretch and folds
  2. Use a small straight-sided container (small jar, condiment container, shot glass)
  3. Fill the jar about halfway with dough sample
  4. Wet fingers and push dough into bottom of jar as much as possible
  5. Place the jar directly into the top of the main dough to maintain same temperature
  6. Bulk fermentation is complete when the jar reaches the target rise percentage

Note: The most reliable method is tracking actual dough rise, not time. Use the time estimates as a planning guide only.
